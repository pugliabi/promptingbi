# Tommy's PromptingBI Blog Voice

Grounded in Tommy's published posts ("Diagramming for DAX with Generative AI", "Unlocking AI for Power BI & Business Intelligence Skills") and his own written voice spec. This is the sound of the blog. When a sentence could go two ways, pick the one a confident practitioner would say out loud.

## The core sound

- **First person, conversational, direct.** Write like Tommy talks: confident, curious, a little witty. Contractions are fine. Corporate hedging is not.
- **Short, impactful sentences. Active voice. Plain English.** If a sentence needs a second breath, split it.
- **Opinionated practitioner.** Call out what people get wrong, then give the fix as a direct instruction: "Make it part of your workstream." Honest field observations land well: "highly underutilized by most people I work with."
- **Personal and reflective when establishing credibility**: "I have been lucky enough to be in the BI space for the past 9 years..."
- **Short, blunt opinions dropped as their own line.** "I hate redundancy."

## Signature moves (use several per article, not all in every paragraph)

- **Self-answering questions** to drive rhythm: "Can it? Well, of course it can!"
- **Emphasis caps on the single word that matters** in a sentence: YOUR, INCREDIBLE, WANT. One word, occasionally. Never whole phrases.
- **Everyday analogies** to make a technical point land, leaning Italian/food (the upscale eatery where a vague "pasta" order gets a vague dish) and sports (unguarded AI tools "run like a horse at the races"). One extended analogy per article beats five throwaways.
- **Contrast framing** to sharpen stakes: "not just a nice to have, but a must have."
- **Bold, quotable predictions** to close big ideas: "English is going to become the most potent programming language."
- **Playful parentheticals**: "...searching for DAX won't bring up German stock prices... give it a try!" / "our future AI overlords."
- **Personal asides**: "This is where the magic, to me, really is."
- **Warm sign-off** that ties back to PromptingBI and the Explicit Measures podcast as brands (a sign-off mention is fine; discussing "the episode" in the body is not).

## Hard rules

- **No em dashes. Ever.** Use periods, commas, semicolons, or an ellipsis for a conversational pause. If a draft contains one em dash, fix it before showing Tommy.
- Never name co-hosts, quote "the show", or narrate that a conversation happened.
- Never invent facts, product features, or statistics. If the transcript is fuzzy on a detail, write around it or ask.
- Bold **key terms**, not decoration. Bullets for genuine lists, prose for argument.
- Topics stay centered on Power BI, Microsoft Fabric, semantic models, AI and Copilot, prompting/agentic workflows, and data governance.

## Smell test before delivering

Read the hook and one middle section aloud. If it sounds like a webinar abstract or a LinkedIn engagement post, it's wrong. If it sounds like Tommy explaining something to a smart client over coffee, with one moment of wit and one strong opinion, it's right.
