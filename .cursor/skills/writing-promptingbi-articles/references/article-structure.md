# Article Structure & Content Rules

## Shape of every post

1. **One angle.** The single strongest idea from the source carries the whole post. Everything off-angle is cut or filed as a future Idea row.
2. **Title**: clear, specific, curiosity-driven. No episode number, no "recap"/"thoughts on" framing. Pattern that works: claim or tension + who it's for ("Motivated Skepticism: Why People Reject Accurate Dashboards (and How to Fix It)").
3. **Hook**: 2–3 sentences opening on the idea itself, as if Tommy sat down to write about it today. Concrete beats abstract: a scene, a failure mode, a number.
4. **Thesis**: the point of view the post argues, stated in one or two lines.
5. **Body**: 3–6 sections with H2/H3 headers. Each section develops ONE supporting idea with Tommy's reasoning, a vivid analogy or example, and a takeaway. Short bold section labels in his style work well (Feeding the Model, Visual Help, Final Result, Takeaways).
6. **Practical application**: what a Power BI / Fabric / BI professional should actually DO with this. Steps, scripts, checklists, rituals. This section is why an MVP blog gets bookmarked.
7. **Close**: a short opinion plus a forward-looking prediction, then a light call to action (subscribe to PromptingBI, keep the conversation going). End the body with a "Key Takeaway" style landing: the smallest next action the reader can take this week.

## Content rules

- Target **1,200–1,800 words**. Skimmable: bold key terms, bullets for real lists.
- Ground every claim in the transcript or in what Tommy said during the walk-the-areas pass. Reframe his spoken examples as written prose; never paste spoken sentences verbatim.
- If the transcript covers several unrelated topics, the strongest becomes this post; list the others at the end of your working notes and file them as Idea rows in the database (not in the published article).
- Finish with a **one-line meta description** (~150 chars, benefit-led, includes a primary keyword) and **5–8 topic tags** drawn from or consistent with the database's Topics options.

## Image prompts

Each Image Ideas entry must be a **complete standalone generation prompt**: subject, setting, mood/style, composition, and any text elements, plus alt text for the site. Assume Tommy pastes it into Grok Imagine unedited. Banner concepts should feel editorial (a scene that dramatizes the tension of the post), not clip-art ("a dashboard with charts").
